Same shape in both scenarios: utterance_id → speakers[] (one or more), with source (human/llm) and confidence. This is what the CLI would ingest.

One interface, one queue. The queue holds whatever needs attention: unlabeled runs on a fresh transcript, low-confidence flags after an LLM pass. A plain key is the fast path: it labels and jumps to the next item. For a mixed utterance, hold ⇧: ⇧2 ⇧3 stack speakers on the selection without jumping, then Enter moves on. Already jumped? ⌫ brings you back. Dashed dividers mark pauses over 2 s in the audio; the default selection stops at them because pauses often mean the speaker changed. They're hints, not truth: this transcript has pauses mid-speaker and speaker changes with no pause. Short mic rows marked "overlap" are backchannels spoken over ongoing speech; the selection flows across them instead of treating them as turn boundaries. For a fused utterance (two speakers, no pause, like the "—no, you go ahead" hand-off), select it and press S to label sentence by sentence; utterance texts are always complete sentences, so sentences are a free sub-unit.
